2. Land Acknowledgement – Reconnaissance des Terres
We acknowledge that we are gathered on the traditional and unceded territory of the Algonquin Anishinaabe Nation. For generations, they have been the stewards of this land, and their deep connection to it continues to this day. We recognize the enduring presence and contributions of the Algonquin people to this region. We also acknowledge the painful history of colonization and the displacement of Indigenous communities from their ancestral lands. 	Establish a Separate Coordinator Policy
	Mover
Proposé
	Dakota
	Seconder
	Daniel
	WHEREAS the current Policy and Procedure Manual includes “Coordinators” under the Manager Policy, despite the fact that Coordinators are distinct in mandate, duration, and function;
WHEREAS Coordinators are typically responsible for planning and executing time-limited projects or major events, and do not require the same year-long operational oversight as Managers;
WHEREAS distinguishing Coordinators in a separate policy section will clarify expectations, improve organizational structure, and support consistent appointment processes;
BE IT RESOLVED THAT a new section titled §4.0 Coordinator Policy be added to the Policy and Procedure Manual, following §3.0 Manager Policy and before §5.0 Commissioner Policy;
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT the following subsections be included in the new Coordinator Policy:
4.1 Purpose
The Coordinator Policy shall outline the responsibilities and structure of Coordinator roles within the Society, which are designed to support time-bound or event-specific mandates.
4.2 Scope
This policy shall apply to all Coordinators appointed to organize specific events, projects, or services under the supervision of a designated Executive.
4.3 Policy
4.3.1 Appointment
Coordinators shall be appointed by the Officers of the Society, upon recommendation by the relevant Executive.
4.3.2 Eligibility
Any active Member of the Society may become a Coordinator provided that the Member is in good standing with the faculty.
4.3.3 Term
Coordinators shall serve for the duration of the event, project, or term as outlined in their mandate, or until they are otherwise removed or replaced by the Officers.
4.3.4 Roles of the 101 Week Coordinator
The 101 Week Coordinator of the Society shall:
1. form and lead the 101 Week Committee;
2. hold office until the conclusion of 101 Week;
3. report to the VP Social with 101 Week related events throughout the summer;
   1. assist the VP Philanthropic with the organization of the philanthropic event during 101 Week;
4. assist in the coordination of 101 Week events throughout the week.
4.3.4.1 Eligibility of the 101 Week Coordinator
To be appointed as 101 Week Coordinator, an active Member must:
1. reside in Ottawa during the summer.
4.3.5 Roles of the Wine and Cheese Coordinator
The Wine and Cheese Coordinator of the Society shall:
1. report to the VP Academic; and
2. help to plan and run one (1) Wine and Cheese in both the Fall and Winter semesters.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT the 101 Week Coordinator and Wine and Cheese Coordinator be removed from the Manager Policy (formerly §3.3.12 and §3.3.13);
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT §3.3.3 of the Manager Policy be amended to remove “with the exception of the Manager of 101 week whose duration of their mandate is defined in section ‎f”.

	Result
Résultat

	Rename and Update the Role of Returning Officer/Godparent to Accountability Officer
	Mover
Proposé
	Aiden
	Seconder
	Ashna
	WHEREAS the current role of Returning Officer/Godparent is titled in a way that lacks clarity and consistency with the Society’s professional governance framework;
WHEREAS the responsibilities of this role should include broader accountability, institutional continuity, and conflict resolution functions;
WHEREAS the Society seeks to strengthen governance and oversight by ensuring this Officer supports both the Chair of the Board and the Executive in maintaining transparency and integrity;
WHEREAS the current placement of the role under the Commissioner Policy does not reflect its governance oversight purpose;
WHEREAS the current wording of §1.01(k) of the By-laws is ambiguous as to whether the Returning Officer/Godparent has voting rights;
BE IT RESOLVED THAT all references to “Returning Officer” in the Policy and Procedure Manual be replaced with “Accountability Officer”;
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT the Accountability Officer be removed from §4.3.6 of the Commissioner Policy;
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT the following be inserted as §1.3.3 of the Board Member Policy:
1.3.3 Accountability Officer
The Accountability Officer shall:
1. report to both the Chair of the Board and the Vice President of Equity;
2. act as a neutral advisor to the Executive and Board by providing institutional context and supporting continuity across terms;
3. assist the Chair of the Board in upholding governance best practices, procedural fairness, and ethical conduct;
4. assist the Chair of the Board in monitoring the fulfillment of executive and director mandates;
5. act as an unbiased mediator for internal conflicts when requested;
6. support or conduct preliminary reviews of alleged policy violations or conflicts when authorized by the Chair or a majority vote of the Board;
7. maintain confidentiality and impartiality in all proceedings and may submit recommendations to the Board as needed; and
8. receive governance-related concerns from Members and advise them on appropriate processes or escalation pathways.
1.3.3.1 Eligibility for Accountability Officer
To be appointed as Accountability Officer, an active Member must:
1. have previously served as an Executive or Chair of the Board.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT §1.01(k) of the By-laws be amended to read:
“Officers” means Executives, Managers, Commissioners, Coordinators, and the Accountability Officer. The Chair of the Board, Secretaries, and the Accountability Officer shall not have voting rights.

Title
Titre
	Amendments to Purpose and Scope Clauses in All Policies
	Mover
Proposé
	Daniel
	Seconder
	Dakota
	WHEREAS the Society has undertaken a comprehensive review of its Policy and Procedure Manual to improve clarity, consistency, and alignment with good governance practices;
WHEREAS several Purpose and Scope sections currently contain vague, inconsistent, or overly narrow language that does not reflect the actual function or application of the respective policies;
WHEREAS clearly defined Purpose and Scope statements support transparency, ease of interpretation, and policy enforcement;
BE IT RESOLVED THAT the Purpose and Scope clauses of the following policies in the Policy and Procedure Manual be amended as follows:
Board Member Policy
Purpose
This policy outlines the responsibilities, expectations, and internal procedures of Board Members and Officers involved in the governance of the Society.
Scope
This policy applies to all individuals serving on the Board of Directors, including the Officers of the Board.
Executive Member Policy
Purpose
This policy outlines the responsibilities, expectations, and structure of the Executives of the Society to ensure clarity and accountability in their roles.
Scope
This policy applies to all individuals serving as Executives of the Society.
Manager Policy
Purpose
This policy outlines the roles, responsibilities, and expectations of Managers elected to assist Executives in carrying out operational tasks.
Scope
This policy applies to all individuals elected or appointed as Managers.
Commissioner Policy
Purpose
This policy outlines the roles and responsibilities of Commissioners and the procedures for their appointment by the Society.
Scope
This policy applies to all individuals appointed as Commissioners by the Officers of the Society.
Committee Policy
Purpose
This policy outlines the responsibilities and structure of Committees established by the Society to support designated mandates.
Scope
This policy applies to all Members appointed to serve on Committees.
Removal from Office Policy
Purpose
This policy outlines the procedures and grounds for the removal of individuals from positions of responsibility within the Society, in order to uphold accountability, integrity, and a safe organizational environment.
Scope
This policy applies to all individuals serving as Officers or Directors of the Society.
Financial Controls Policy
Purpose
This policy outlines the financial controls and procedures governing the use of Society funds, in order to ensure accountability, transparency, and proper stewardship of resources.
Scope
This policy applies to all Officers, Directors, and individuals authorized to handle or oversee the Society’s financial transactions.
Transition Policy
Purpose
This policy outlines the responsibilities and procedures for outgoing Officers and Directors to ensure an effective and accountable transition of institutional knowledge and duties.
Scope
This policy applies to all individuals serving in elected or appointed leadership positions within the Society who are expected to transition their role to a successor.
Locker Rental Policy
Purpose
This policy outlines the procedures, responsibilities, and rules governing the rental and use of Society-owned lockers.
Scope
This policy applies to all Members of the Society and any other university students authorized to use the Society’s locker rental services.
Equipment Rental Policy
Purpose
This policy outlines the rules, procedures, and responsibilities related to the rental and use of equipment owned by the Society.
Scope
This policy applies to all Members of the Society and any individuals authorized to rent its equipment.
Inclusivity Policy
Purpose
This policy establishes standards and expectations to promote inclusivity, equity, and safety at all Society-affiliated events, activities, and spaces.
Scope
This policy applies to all Society events, initiatives, and physical or virtual spaces under the authority of the Society.
Procurement Policy
Scope
This policy applies to all Officers, Directors, committee members, and any individuals authorized to make or oversee purchases on behalf of the Society, including goods, services, rentals, leases, subscriptions, and contractual commitments.
All procurement activities must comply with the Financial Controls Policy and align with the approved budget.

	Quorum Threshold and Procedural Integrity
	Mover
Proposé
	Aiden
	Seconder
	Zoe
	WHEREAS the current quorum threshold of 50% plus one can at times impede the Board’s ability to conduct regular business;
WHEREAS reducing the quorum threshold to one-third (⅓) of Directors entitled to vote strikes a more practical balance between operational feasibility and governance legitimacy;
WHEREAS the existing clause allowing meetings to continue after quorum is lost during the meeting undermines the legitimacy of decisions made without adequate voting representation;
WHEREAS proxies are not entitled to vote and therefore should not be counted toward quorum, as quorum should reflect the presence of actual voting Directors;
BE IT RESOLVED THAT §6.04 of the By-laws be amended to read as follows:
“Quorum at any meeting of the Board shall be one-third (⅓) of the Directors entitled to vote at the meeting, or ten (10) Directors, whichever is greater.”
