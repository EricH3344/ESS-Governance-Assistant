Land Acknowledgement - Reconnaissance des terres


* We acknowledge that we are gathered on the traditional and unceded territory of the Algonquin Anishinaabe Nation. For generations, they have been the stewards of this land, and their deep connection to it continues to this day. We recognize the enduring presence and contributions of the Algonquin people to this region. We also acknowledge the painful history of colonization and the displacement of Indigenous communities from their ancestral lands. We commit ourselves to learning, understanding, and honoring the Algonquin Nation's history and culture. WHEREAS The Society’s spirit and community engagement are currently concentrated around 101 Week;

BE IT RESOLVED THAT a new Spirit Commissioner position be established;

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT the Spirit Commissioner uphold the competitive engineering spirit of 101 week and its related teams throughout the academic year 
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED THAT this role will be probationary for the duration of the 2026 winter semester, and a review will be conducted by the Chair, President, and VP Social to determine whether the role should be amended to policy.
