2. Land Acknowledgement – Reconnaissance des Terres
We acknowledge that we are gathered on the traditional and unceded territory of the Algonquin Anishinaabe Nation. For generations, they have been the stewards of this land, and their deep connection to it continues to this day. We recognize the enduring presence and contributions of the Algonquin people to this region. We also acknowledge the painful history of colonization and the displacement of Indigenous communities from their ancestral lands. We commit ourselves to learning, understanding, and honoring the Algonquin Nation's history and culture. We recognize the importance of working collaboratively to create a more just and equitable future for all who call this land home.

	Motion for Removal of the Vice President of Sustainable Initiatives
Motion de destitution de la Vice-présidente aux initiatives durables
	Mover
Proposé
	VP Internal
	Seconder
	VP Phil
	WHEREAS the Vice President of Sustainable Initiatives has not attended or submitted updates for any Officer or Board meetings since the beginning of the current mandate;
WHEREAS Executives are expected to fulfill both their Executive and Director responsibilities, including attendance and regular updates at Officer and Board meetings;
WHEREAS the Vice President of Sustainable Initiatives has not communicated any reasons for their repeated absences, nor requested to be excused or provided justification after the fact, despite multiple opportunities to do so;
WHEREAS the Vice President of Sustainable Initiatives has failed to demonstrate any meaningful progress on the responsibilities outlined in their mandate, including but not limited to establishing or maintaining ties with sustainability-related organizations at the University of Ottawa, supporting internal sustainability-related events and affairs, consulting on relevant initiatives, or conducting any visible work under the sustainability portfolio;
WHEREAS the Society recently adopted a Special Rule on attendance expectations that, while not retroactive, clarifies longstanding expectations around participation, and the repeated unexcused absences fall outside any reasonable interpretation of acceptable engagement;
WHEREAS such conduct constitutes a “failure to follow the regulations of the Constitution without just cause,” as defined under the Removal from Office Policy;
WHEREAS continued inaction and disengagement in the role serves neither the Society nor its membership, and undermines the expectations of accountability and responsibility in holding an Executive and Director position;
BE IT RESOLVED THAT Leila Smaili be removed from the position of Vice President of Sustainable Initiatives in accordance with the Removal from Office Policy.
